# Growth-regime recovery ranking

*Two regimes, because recovery works differently. HIGH-GROWTH names grow INTO the multiple — anchored to JUSTIFIED PEG (a 60x P/E on 50% growth is PEG ~1.2, cheap), so recovery time is the PEG earn-back clock and they rank high when likely to reclaim their level within the 18-month mandate. DIVERSIFIED/SAFE names recover via buyback + adjusted-P/E earn-back, ranked by reward-vs-S&P. Research model, not investment advice.*

## How to read it
- **High-growth recovery = PEG earn-back**: a fast grower's fair multiple is set by growth (PEG≈1.4×g), so a name trading below that floor has ~no gap and reclaims fast.
- **Rank-high gate**: P(recover≤18m) ≥ 75% (the user's threshold) OR a PEG earn-back inside the 18-month mandate. At today's elevated-stress calibration the conditional MC recovery rarely clears 75% on its own, so the PEG earn-back time is the binding, more discriminating signal for the high-growth bucket.
- **For high-growth ETFs** this holds only if the theme's TAM/SAM stays intact and growth persists/accelerates after the crash (secular). A cyclical-TAM basket (DRAM/memory) dips-then-rebounds — execution-diversified, but on a cyclical demand curve.
